Course Description

Python is one of the most popular programming langauges of the 21st century. It used for developing desktop applications, websites, and web applications. It is also the language to learn for becoming a data scientist. Functional programming (FP) is a way of thinking about software construction that emphasizes functional purity and immutability. Due to the ease of testing, verification and debugging of functional code, the use of functional programming is continuously on the rise in the industry. According to Stack OverFlow Developer Survey 2019, two of highest paying programming languages enforce the functional approach.

The goal of this course is to teach programming in Python (3.8) and the functional programming (FP) design methodology, with a focus on hands-on learning and industry relevance.

Information about the content, teaching, and evaluation of the course is available at the syllabus page. The course is not being offered as an online course, and the notebooks, assignments, lecture slides and other resources are provided only for your personal reference and entertainment purposes. They are not (as of Jan 2020) part of course requirement for any degree-bearing university program. If you wish to include parts of the course or assignments in your curriculum, please contact the instructors, and we'd be glad to help you out.
